# Launch playbook — first customers

Use this alongside the live site. Replace placeholder contact info in `src/lib/site.ts` before going live.

## Google Business Profile (GBP)

1. Claim or create a profile with your legal business name (e.g. **Seattle Wall Co.**) and matching website URL.
2. Categories: start with **Print shop** or **Sign shop** (pick the closest fit); add secondary categories only if accurate.
3. Service area: set Seattle + Eastside cities you actually serve (match `site.serviceAreas`).
4. Photos: upload 10+ owned install shots as you complete jobs; avoid stock long-term.
5. Reviews: after each successful install, send a short review request with a direct GBP link.

## Pilot offer (documented case study)

Target buyers who already budget for space: commercial interior designers, retail GCs, coworking operators, event venues.

**Email skeleton**

> Subject: Pilot wall graphics install in Seattle  
> Body: We’re onboarding two flagship installs this quarter with a documented before/after and fast turnaround. If you have a lobby or retail feature wall in [neighborhood], reply with photos and rough dimensions—we’ll respond with a scoped range and timeline.

Trade: **modest discount** for permission to photograph, logo/credit on site, and a short testimonial.

## Neighborhood visibility

1. Pick 1–2 visible local partners (coffee, gym, studio) for near-cost installs.
2. Add discreet plaque or counter card: “Printed by [Your Brand]” with QR to site.
3. Ask for warm intro to the building’s marketing lead.

## Referral partners

Local vinyl/sign shops without vertical UV capability: offer **10–15% referral fee** or reciprocal overflow work. Keep a one-pager PDF mirroring your web pricing bands.

## Paid search (later)

After GBP + 3 real reviews, test a small geo-limited campaign on terms like “custom wall graphics Seattle”. Send traffic to `/contact` with a “Book a site visit” hook.

## Quote template (copy into your CRM)

- Wall location + dimensions (field verified?)  
- Surface type + prep needs  
- Access: lift, hours, dock, parking  
- Creative: client files vs. design hours  
- Timeline + rush fees  
- Range (reference site tiers) vs. fixed price after scope lock  
- Deposit, balance schedule, change order policy  

## Review request (SMS/email)

> Thanks again for trusting us with [location]. If you have 60 seconds, a Google review helps other Seattle teams find us: [GBP link]. Happy to touch up any punch-list items—just reply here.
